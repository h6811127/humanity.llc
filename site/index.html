The idea

Physical software objects

Stickers, clothing, signs, and cards are not just printed media. Each QR is a deployed software endpoint in the world — status, permissions, and revocation can change after the ink dries.

  • Printing is deployment, not a dead link
  • Scan interacts with live state, not a frozen file
  • One primitive powers benches, wearables, markets, and flyers

Same idea elsewhere

One primitive: a physical thing points at current signed status, not a permanent file. QR works on any camera today.

  • Poster or flyer Mutual-aid or event contact. Revoke when the run ends, not when stickers fade.
  • Sticker on merch Scan shows active or revoked; a stolen batch can be killed without reprinting the line
  • Member badge or card Club or co-op asks for live membership state, not a static plastic claim
