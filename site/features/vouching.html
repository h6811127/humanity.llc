About this feature

Why it matters ›

In an age of synthetic agents, communities need accountability-who put their name on this profile under published rules-not a central iris registry. Three eligible vouchers sign public Ed25519 vouches; the card becomes Vouched Human. Not legal ID. Not proof the person holding a sticker owns the card (use live control when that matters).

Design decisions ›

Own the verb vouch: sign and publish a revocable attestation. Verifiable documents on the network; default statement template; private notes never POSTed. Threshold (3), quota (5/year), 90-day wait for new vouchers, one active vouch per pair. Scan: Issue vouch, Sign as… when keys are not loaded, count + recency-no full social graph on first screen.

Safety · privacy · security ›

Signed payloads, nonce replay protection, voucher eligibility checks. Documented threats include Sybil cliques (minimum 4 mutual vouchers), rotating rings, stolen keys, and integrator over-trust-mitigated by quotas, wait periods, operator audit flags, and revocation. See docs/VOUCH_THREAT_MODEL.md.

Limits ›

Does not prove legal identity, global uniqueness, voucher honesty, or liveness at sign time. Registration ≠ Vouched Human. Steward on the network ≠ signing key in this tab - see docs/KEYS_CARDS_AND_VERIFICATION.md and docs/VOUCH_TRUST_POSITIONING.md.

Future directions ›

Integrator trust-policy helpers (min vouches, recency), steward review queue UI, ceremony credentials (separate path), cross-operator federation. Positioning doc: docs/VOUCH_TRUST_POSITIONING.md.
