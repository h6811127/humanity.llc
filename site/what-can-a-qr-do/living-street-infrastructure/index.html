Design space · bench

Living street infrastructure

Not “QR on a bench.” A bench that is addressable - with state, history, and meaning anyone can read by scanning.

Research walkthrough. One object, five directions.

1 · Public history

People leave tiny notes. The bench becomes a living guestbook.

  • I sat here after my interview.
  • Best sunset at 7:42.
  • Someone proposed here.
  • This bench needs repair.
  • Free coffee nearby.

2 · Civic sensor, not surveillance

The object reports public state - not who sat on it.

  • Clean
  • Broken
  • Occupied recently
  • Needs maintenance
  • Event nearby
  • Community notice active
  • Revoked or invalid sticker

Parks, campuses, neighborhoods, co-ops - shared truth without tracking people.

3 · Local belonging

A live page turns a bench into a tiny landmark. The neighborhood collectively knows:

  • the chess bench
  • the grief bench
  • the sunset bench
  • the free-book bench
  • the first-date bench
  • the mutual-aid bench

Physical places get a social identity.

4 · Lightweight games

The bench becomes a checkpoint - no app required:

  • Scan five benches Unlock a route across the city
  • Claim for your faction Territory that can change when state updates
  • Leave a clue Unlock a local story on the next scan
  • Prove you visited Camera scan is enough

Where “tiny public storage primitive” gets interesting.

5 · Programmable public space

The bench can answer, right now:

  • What is happening here?
  • Who maintains this?
  • Is this part of an event?
  • Is this object trusted?
  • Has this sticker been revoked?
  • What did people leave behind?

The benefit is lightweight, revocable, public memory in the physical world. Addressable → state → history, permissions, games, maintenance, identity, and meaning.
