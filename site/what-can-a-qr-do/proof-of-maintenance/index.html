Proof of maintenance

A QR on a defibrillator, fire extinguisher, water filter, vending machine, or generator resolves to last checked, current status, report issue — a public maintenance digest, not a hidden spreadsheet.

Boring, valuable

Small buildings, co-ops, and pop-ups cannot always run a full CMMS. A sticker is enough when the question is narrow: is this safe to use right now?

Signed check-ins

A maintainer records inspection with an organizer key — not anonymous crowd edits.

The object shows a digest of recent signed updates: who checked, when, and whether the unit is in or out of service. Full logs can stay private; the scan shows what strangers need.

Infrastructure becomes a checkable maintenance object in the hallway. That is the magic.
