Memory & place

Not scan to open a website. More like scan to hear what this place remembers.

Research, not shipping yet.

Invisible history

Every place has history that never becomes official. Normally it disappears unless someone writes it down.

  • People had first dates there.
  • Someone sat there after bad news.
  • A band played there before they were known.
  • A protest started there.
  • A family owned that shop for 40 years.
  • Someone left town from that bus stop. Someone came back.

A live QR lets ordinary places hold ordinary human memory.

What the QR can hold

A tiny public archive attached to the real world:

  • one audio story
  • timeline of events
  • photos from different years
  • anonymous notes
  • what happened here
  • local folklore
  • maintenance history
  • memorial messages
  • artist commentary
  • neighborhood updates
  • old maps
  • memories unlocked over time

Examples

  • The Sunset Bench 83 memories · oldest 2019 · latest audio today · status active · prompt: “What were you thinking about when you sat here?”
  • A mural Artist story · making-of photos · community comments · neighborhood timeline · restored 2026 · verified by the artist
  • A closed storefront Business history · former owner interview · community memories · what is coming next
  • A trail marker Indigenous history · ecology notes · seasonal observations · visitor reflections · audio guide

Emotional depth

The internet becomes a thin memory layer over reality. The place gets:

  • a voice
  • a past
  • a timeline
  • a community
  • a feeling
  • a record

Not centered on a person’s profile — centered on the place.

Place-based memory capsules

A creator or community places a QR somewhere meaningful. People scan for text, audio, image, timeline, or prompt. Memory accumulates — and stays live:

  • updated
  • moderated
  • revoked
  • transferred
  • archived
  • marked unofficial
  • community verified
  • disabled if abused

Why live objects matter

Usually

  • Plaques stay static
  • QR links rot
  • Social posts vanish in feeds

Memory capsule

  • Attached to the place
  • Live and accumulating
  • Revocable when needed

A human memory city

Not surveillance smart city. A library of lived experience. Certain places can quietly say:

  • people were here
  • something happened here
  • this mattered to someone
  • this place is still alive

Memory & place makes reality feel annotated by the people who lived through it. That is the magic.
