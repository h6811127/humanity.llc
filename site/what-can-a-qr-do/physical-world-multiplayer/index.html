Physical-world multiplayer

The city stops being a backdrop and becomes a playable map. A sticker on a bench, pole, mural, café window, trail marker, or hallway is a tiny game object — scan to change the state of the world, not just visit a page.

Maps in real space

Traditional games have maps, capture points, quests, factions, inventory, checkpoints, and lore. Humanity-style QRs let you place those mechanics in public space.

What a sticker can be

  • unclaimed
  • captured by Blue faction
  • vulnerable after 8 PM
  • scanned by 42 players
  • contains clue 3 of 7
  • revoked by creator
  • part of tonight’s route
  • lore updated after last capture

The QR is not the game. The QR is the physical handle for a live game object.

Ordinary places, enchanted

  • A bench The Western Gate · Red faction · last captured 14 min ago · one hidden message · unlocks next coordinate after 5 scans
  • A mural Active quest node · chapter 2 of local lore · unlockable after sunset · verified by the original artist
  • A café window Neutral territory · safe zone · meetup point · temporary merchant node · event check-in
  • A trail marker Checkpoint 4 · clue unlocked · path split activated · faction bonus applied

The city becomes a multiplayer board.

City-scale example

Faction games with revocable public objects. A weekend in Cedar Rapids: 50 QR nodes across parks, cafés, murals, benches, and bike trails. Three factions. Capture, defend, reveal lore, unlock missions.

  • Red controls NewBo
  • Blue controls Czech Village
  • Green found the hidden river node
  • Node 17 compromised — revoked
  • Final event unlocks at 30 captures

Not a scavenger hunt. A lightweight operating system for public play.

Why it matters

People get a reason to notice their environment:

  • this bench matters
  • this mural has a story
  • this alley has a clue
  • this café is part of the game
  • this sticker is alive
  • this city has hidden state

Public space feels interactive, social, and alive — without an app, headset, or expensive hardware. That is the magic.
