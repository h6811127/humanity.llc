Physical-world multiplayer

The city stops being a backdrop and becomes a playable season. A bench, mural, café window, alley arch, stairwell, or trail marker can act like a live quest object — scan to change public world state, not just open a page.

Research, not shipping yet. High imagination, low surveillance.

Respect the data policy

Maximal creativity does not require maximal collection. The operator can support the fantasy by publishing object truth while refusing the usual scan surveillance.

May be public

  • Which faction holds a node
  • Whether a route, chapter, or sanctuary is live
  • Whether a marker is active, paused, or revoked

May be signed

  • Artist notes, maintainer notices, and lore drops
  • Repair confirmations and event state transitions
  • Rules that change the map without changing the ink

Must not become normal

  • No per-scan trails, location history, or device fingerprinting
  • No phone or email required just to participate in the idea
  • No hidden expansion into silent access logging

Read the operator data policy: no scan analytics by default, and any future minimal logs require published governance.

Play that helps upkeep

The best version makes people care more about place. Discovery can reward attention and stewardship without pretending players can certify safety-critical truth.

Discovery

  • Find the rain-garden drain, fountain light, mural lamp, or trail marker
  • Learn what state the object should show
  • Earn lore for noticing the city closely

Care loop

  • Report “sign missing,” “light out,” or “marker cracked”
  • Maintainer-signed updates can pause a node cleanly
  • Repair reactivates the route and opens the next chapter

Hard boundary

  • Players do not mark emergency or safety equipment safe
  • Game rewards discovery, not risky shortcuts
  • Maintenance status remains the public truth

Pair with proof of maintenance when the object carries real-world service or safety state.

Why this feels new

People get a reason to notice the city differently:

  • this bench matters tonight
  • this mural is part of a larger story
  • this alley is a threshold
  • this café is sanctuary, not just commerce
  • this marker is alive right now
  • this city has hidden state without hidden tracking

Public space feels interactive, social, and enchanted — no headset, no proprietary app, no scan-surveillance heatmap. That is the magic.
