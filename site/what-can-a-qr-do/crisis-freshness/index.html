What the scan page resolves first

  • Resource routing Nearby pantry, fridge, warming center, and current pickup windows.
  • Batch freshness Made time, expiry, allergens, recall state, and host-kitchen verification.
  • No tracking required The resource page updates for every old card without building a dossier of who scanned.

Who signs what

Coalition

  • Which resources are available tonight
  • Addresses, hours, and pickup windows
  • Which card series is still trusted

Kitchen or pantry host

  • Made and expires times
  • Allergen notes
  • Recall or discard instructions

Site maintainer

  • Fridge working or down
  • Generator or cooler maintenance
  • Equipment warnings outrank “available food” copy

What the combined loop enables

Resource card

  • One wallet card lists pantry and fridge options nearby
  • Coalition updates the live resource map in one place
  • Old cards in pockets stay useful

Batch tags

  • Each soup, tray, or produce box keeps a separate freshness object
  • Safe-to-take state can change without changing the aid card
  • Allergen and recall notes stay batch-specific

Protection

  • Recall disappears unsafe food from the live list
  • Fridge outage can hide only the affected items
  • No scan history needed to keep people safer

Pair with crisis resource cards, food freshness tags, and proof of maintenance.

The crisis card can route people to help, but the food authority stays with the kitchen and the equipment authority stays with the host.
