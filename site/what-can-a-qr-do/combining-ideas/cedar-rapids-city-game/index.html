Cedar Rapids city game

A weekend game spreads across NewBo, Czech Village, the river trail, and the skywalk. Each node resolves to a different card chain: district control, relay bulletin, steward status, and optional local lore.

Research demo, not shipping behavior yet. No scan analytics, no device fingerprinting, no identity trails.

What this scan demo is exploring

Different nodes, different chains

  • Relay arches show district control plus current team bulletin
  • Bridges show compromise state plus route rules
  • Lookouts can add weather, flood, or lore overlays

Communication points

  • Controlling a node unlocks one rotating signed team message
  • The resolver stores the bulletin, not who pulled it
  • Neutral zones can expose public truce or meetup windows

Compromise without surveillance

  • Spies can jam or poison a relay by publishing a signed compromise notice
  • The scan shows current trust state, not a per-player access log
  • Rekeying clears the relay without keeping scan history

Game theory: make cooperation pay

The Cedar Rapids version works best when the rules reward coordination more than exploitation. The city should feel like a shared puzzle where trust, timing, and scarcity change what objects reveal.

Public goods + anti-hoarding

  • Some objects wake only after enough people contribute
  • Example: 20 anonymous scans unlock the next clue
  • First finder gets a clue, but the object evolves only after 5 more people scan it

Trust / vouch mechanics

  • Deeper paths unlock when previous objects, businesses, or community members vouch
  • Example: this clue requires a vouch from a NewBo object
  • That creates legitimacy without needing legal identity

Prisoner’s dilemma design

  • Selfish play gives a small short-term reward
  • Cooperation unlocks a larger shared reward
  • Example: reveal a clue privately now, or leave it hidden so the group unlocks a better ending

Scarcity without surveillance

  • Use time windows, expiring clues, rotating QR states, or limited-use capabilities
  • Example: this object can only issue 25 passes before sunset
  • Scarcity comes from the object, not from tracking people

Sybil resistance

  • Rate limits
  • Proof-of-control challenges
  • Local physical codes, one-time signed tokens, business-issued vouches, and device-local limits

Coordination games

  • Some puzzles require people to visit different neighborhoods and combine information
  • Example: Czech Village, NewBo, and Greene Square each reveal one fragment
  • Together they unlock the next state

Best version: not points and badges, but a city-scale coordination game where trust, timing, scarcity, and cooperation change what physical objects reveal.

Data policy fit

No scan tracking.

This demo assumes no scan analytics, no phone-number requirement, and no device fingerprinting. Team relay copy is a current signed bulletin, not a record of who visited. If a node is compromised, the scan shows the current trust warning instead of exposing a scan trail.

Public stewards can still publish closures, flood warnings, or cleanup holds when real space takes priority. That keeps the game playful without turning the resolver into a surveillance system.

The city becomes a playable relay map: territory, rumors, weather, and truce windows all ride on clear authority boundaries.
