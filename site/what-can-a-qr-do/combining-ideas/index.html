Stack the claims, not the authority

A useful scan can combine routing, safety, freshness, and membership without pretending one signer owns all of it.

The scan page should feel like one coherent answer, but the claims behind it stay narrow: the game signs points, the maintainer signs safety, the kitchen signs food, the organizer signs access.

Three rules for strong combinations

  • Keep every claim narrow Each signer should answer one checkable question, not “everything about this card.”
  • Update the stream, not the print One physical mark survives moves, recalls, fixes, and schedule changes.
  • Let the most important warning win Freshness, safety, and revocation must outrank delight, rewards, or discovery copy.

What makes the stack trustworthy

Weak combination

  • One operator signs game points, safety, and food state
  • Fun copy hides a warning or recall
  • Every update requires reprinting or manual cleanup

Strong combination

  • Different claims stay separately signed and revocable
  • The scan page merges them into one readable answer
  • Safety and freshness always outrank engagement

Best combos keep authority clear: the game can reward discovery, but only the maintainer signs safety; the crisis card can show food, but the kitchen signs freshness.
