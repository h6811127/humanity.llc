Lifecycle

Player finds it

  • Scan adds checkpoint 4 to their map
  • Safety status reads “in service”
  • Hint teaches what good condition looks like

Player reports pad expired

  • Report stream opens · no points awarded
  • Maintainer pings, signs out-of-service
  • Map shows node paused, route alternates

Fix verified

  • Maintainer re-signs in-service
  • Game stream re-opens checkpoint
  • Player who reported gets “fixed” badge — no safety claim attached

The game can reward discovery. It cannot ever reward declaring equipment safe.

Rendering rules

Visual hierarchy follows authority — not the other way around.

  • Safety stream renders first, always Even if the game stream is the “cool” part of the scan
  • Stale safety stream silences game stream If the maintainer key has not signed in N days, the checkpoint is collapsed to “status unknown”
  • No scan log retained Game points are scored on the player’s device or game-org server, never “who scanned where” on the resolver

Public play helps people notice the boring infrastructure. It must never become the safety authority.
