What changes over time

Vendor moves or sells out

  • Truck relocates to 4th & Elm lot
  • Same printed QR on truck — scan shows new location
  • Menu marks three more items sold out
  • Vendor updates one state object, not reprinting

After service → surplus

  • Unsold stew becomes a freshness-tagged donation
  • Kitchen signs safe-to-take window
  • Local resource card can point people to it
  • Surplus tag expires, stew listing disappears

Fake or revoked

  • Not the official vendor placard
  • Organizer revokes the stall QR
  • Menu and surplus tags on this vendor go dark
  • Other vendors unaffected

Three streams, one pass

  • Co-op organizer key Signs vendor presence, booth assignment, membership status — never signs food safety
  • Vendor key Signs the menu: available, sold out, prices, allergen notes — updates without reprinting table tents
  • Kitchen key Signs freshness per batch: made, expires, allergens, recall — especially for surplus and donation tags

What the scan page shows

  • vendor: active / inactive
  • booth assignment
  • payment types
  • menu: available items
  • menu: sold out
  • allergen notes
  • surplus: safe-to-take
  • surplus: expires at
  • batch recalled
  • co-op verified
  • member pricing

Not a marketplace app

Usually

  • Markets → third-party app
  • Menus → static PDF or web page
  • Surplus → food-rescue platform
  • Each requires accounts and tracking

This

  • One scan, three signed streams
  • Vendor updates, shoppers see
  • Surplus becomes a tagged donation
  • No shared dossier across roles

The co-op pass, the menu, and the surplus tag are three live objects from three signers — visible on one scan, updatable without reprinting, and revocable independently.
