Authority boundary on the page

The organizer is allowed to say

Which stalls are active, where the market moved, what pass is valid, and whether a booth is approved today.

The vendor or kitchen is allowed to say

Menu items, sold-out state, service window, freshness window, allergens, and surplus safety.

The page should never imply

That market membership alone certifies every prepared-food item as safe, current, or allergen-reviewed.

Morning market

  • Scan pass -> find active stall
  • Open menu shows what is real right now
  • Member pass can unlock co-op pricing without becoming a giant app

After service

  • Prepared food flips from menu inventory to surplus inventory
  • Same card can route to pickup without changing the printed pass
  • Freshness window remains narrow and revocable

Failure to avoid

  • Organizer signing menu details they do not control
  • Vendor claiming prepared-food safety after the kitchen has changed status
  • Overloading one card until nobody knows who is accountable

A good market pass does not become a mega-platform. It stays a small coordination shell that hands off to the right signer for menu and surplus truth.
