Authority boundary on the page

The wallet card is allowed to say

Where food is, when pickup happens, which hotline is active, and when the list was updated.

The kitchen is allowed to say

Made when, expires when, allergens, recall state, do not eat, safe to take.

The page should never imply

That a coalition resource card alone certifies every tray in a fridge as safe forever.

Live aid routing

  • Old cards in circulation still show the current pantry entrance
  • Community fridge can appear or disappear from the list in one signed update
  • The scanner sees the answer without an account

Food safety stays narrow

  • Kitchen updates batch freshness without reissuing the wallet card
  • Recall removes a batch from the safe list immediately
  • Allergen notes travel with the batch, not vague resource copy

Failure to avoid

  • Scan analytics that reveal help-seeking behavior
  • One operator claiming authority over every kitchen in town
  • Printed trust language that outlives freshness truth

Useful crisis infrastructure is not one giant page of promises. It is a current, layered answer that routes to help while keeping freshness truth where it belongs.
