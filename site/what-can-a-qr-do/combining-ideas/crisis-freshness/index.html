What changes over time

Pantry moved

  • Coalition updates hours and address
  • Every old wallet card shows the new site on next scan
  • Individual food tags keep batch-level safety
  • One wallet card, many live resource claims

Batch recalled

  • Kitchen signs recall on soup batch
  • Soup disappears from safe list instantly
  • Rice tray still shows safe — different batch
  • No scan history needed to protect people

Fridge out of service

  • Fridge maintainer signs: temperature check failed
  • All batches in that fridge flip to hold
  • Resource card still shows other pantry options
  • Kitchen signs food; maintainer signs equipment

Three streams, one wallet card

  • Coalition key Signs shelter, pantry, clinic, and crisis-line listings — updates when hours or addresses change
  • Kitchen key Signs each food batch: made, expires, allergens, recall status — updates per-batch, not per-card
  • Equipment maintainer key Signs fridge and generator status — separate from food safety, separate from resource routing

What the scan page shows

  • shelter: open / full
  • pantry hours today
  • crisis line: active
  • food batch: made at
  • food batch: expires at
  • allergens per batch
  • recalled batch
  • fridge status
  • last updated
  • verified by coalition
  • verified by kitchen

What this refuses

Not this

  • Crisis people as marketing assets
  • Scan analytics on a shelter card
  • KYC to read a hotline list
  • Platform tracking who asked for food

This

  • Coalition updates resources, kitchen updates food
  • No account to scan
  • Revocable series
  • Accurate help, not surveillance dressed as aid

The crisis card shows food that exists tonight — and the kitchen that made it is the one signing freshness, not the platform, not the card issuer.
