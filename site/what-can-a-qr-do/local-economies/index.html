Trust at human scale

A QR on a vendor table, member card, food box, or bulletin board is a small live coordination object — not a whole marketplace app.

  • Narrow questions Is this vendor approved? Is pickup still open? Is this need verified?
  • Public status, not spreadsheets Replace paper badges, group-chat screenshots, and stale lists with one checkable object

Co-op and market passes

Just enough infrastructure — trust, status, updates, and revoke without a bloated platform.

A local organizer issues live QR objects for members, vendors, booths, pickup points, supply bins, and mutual-aid requests. Each resolves to active, expired, fulfilled, or revoked — visible on the next scan.

  • Organizer-verified, revocable Fake stalls and lost member cards can be killed without reprinting every placard
  • Not a login honeypot No identity warehouse · no scan analytics farm · coordination through physical objects people already use
  • Separate passes per role Vendor placard ≠ member card ≠ supply-bin route — each object carries only the claim that matters there

A lightweight operating system for small local economies — not Amazon for farmers markets.
