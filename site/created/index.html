Object lifecycle

Revoke is not “delete the sticker.” It is one state transition after the object is in the world — same idea as expiry or (later) claimed or recovered. Humanity stores object state, not who scanned.

  • Active — scan shows live manifesto or pilot layout (status plate, lost item, etc.).
  • Expiry — time runs out on this credential (set at create). Scan shows expired; you can still revoke early.
  • Revoke this QR — one pointer turns off. Other QRs on the same card can stay active.
  • Disable card — whole profile offline; every active QR stops resolving as active.
  • Organizer revoke — coalition key registered at create; organizer revoke without your owner key.

Printed QRs always keep profile ID and QR ID in the URL. Transitions change what the resolver returns — not the ink.

Future transitions (after next scan, claimed, redirect) stay object-state-only — no scan tracking on the operator.
