Where this goes

Activity streams, not a social feed

Today a scan returns current object state — active, revoked, vouched, control proven. The longer arc is richer: one physical QR as a handle for multiple signed update channels controlled by different accountable keys.

  • A game stream — faction holds, route windows, season lore
  • A maintainer stream — repair notices, closures, safety pauses
  • An artist or place stream — history, commentary, local canon

Each stream has an owner and honest limits. Maintenance and safety truth wins when channels conflict. This is how benches, murals, and door plates become physical software objects with living history — without building another attention feed or scan-tracking graph.

Card vs QR

  • Humanity Card One signed profile: handle, manifesto, verification. The container for trust state.
  • QR credential One physical mark. Each sticker, wristband, or door plate gets its own qr_id. Revoke one without killing the card or sibling QRs.

How the mark stores information

The QR is not a database. It encodes a short URL. The camera decodes the pattern; your phone opens the link; the network returns current signed status.

The squares never hold your manifesto or keys — only where to ask. Revoking changes the answer, not the ink.

Stop thinking of the QR as a link. Think of it as a persistent physical pointer, a distributed state object, and a tiny public storage primitive — then the design space opens.

Combining ideas

The strong patterns stack: one QR can stay a simple scan while the card behind it pulls from several signed, revocable streams.

Games + maintenance

  • Players find AED, filter, and trail-marker nodes
  • Maintainer-signed status stays separate from game points
  • Fix verified → local map unlocks the next route

Crisis cards + freshness

  • Wallet aid card lists nearby pantry and fridge options
  • Each food batch keeps made, expires, allergen, recall state
  • Resource page updates without tracking who scanned

Markets + menus + surplus

  • Co-op pass links to today’s stalls and live menus
  • Prepared-food tags mark safe-to-take surplus
  • Vendor updates one state object instead of reprinting

Cedar Rapids city game

  • Different nodes resolve to different relay and district chains
  • Controlled nodes can broadcast team bulletins without scan trails
  • Spy compromises surface as trust warnings, then clear on rekey

Best combos keep authority clear: the game can reward discovery, but only the maintainer signs safety; the crisis card can show food, but the kitchen signs freshness.
