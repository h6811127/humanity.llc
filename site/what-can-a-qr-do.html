What can a QR do?

Once a mark points at live network status, not a frozen URL, the physical world becomes programmable.

The live product today is create → scan → revoke. Everything below is research and imagination built on that primitive.

Card vs QR

  • Humanity Card One signed profile: handle, manifesto, verification. The container for trust state.
  • QR credential One physical mark. Each sticker, wristband, or door plate gets its own qr_id. Revoke one without killing the card or sibling QRs.

How the mark stores information

The QR is not a database. It encodes a short URL. The camera decodes the pattern; your phone opens the link; the network returns current signed status.

The squares never hold your manifesto or keys — only where to ask. Revoking changes the answer, not the ink.

Stop thinking of the QR as a link. Think of it as a persistent physical pointer, a distributed state object, and a tiny public storage primitive — then the design space opens.

Ideas on live QRs

  • Living street infrastructure Community message nodes, local histories, evolving art trails — a bench becomes an addressable social object.
  • Physical-world multiplayer Territory, scavenger hunts, faction wars — capture points and evolving lore across a city because state changes after placement.
  • Civic & protest infrastructure Update meeting points, rotate safety copy, revoke misinformation — without reprinting flyers.
  • Wearable legitimacy Patches and wristbands that resolve to affiliation, event access, or rotating pseudonyms.
  • Local economies Markets and co-ops: membership passes, vendor trust, mutual-aid routing without a giant app stack.
  • Physical software objects Stickers and clothing as deployable entities — printing becomes deployment; QRs are programmable references in the world.
  • Memory & place Stories, audio, and timelines attached to real locations — a persistent memory address in reality.
