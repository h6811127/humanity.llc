Wake the city

Over one weekend, public benches, river markers, relay arches, and café windows show live signed state — who holds a relay, which route is awake, whether a temporary drop unlocked the next clue. No app. No account. No scan tracking.

Draft rules page. Not linked from the public site until season launch. Dates and live nodes will be filled when CITY_GAME_ENABLED=1 on production.

What a scan proves

  • Public object truth Which faction holds a relay, whether a sanctuary is live, whether a temp drop expired, whether a marker is compromised or paused.
  • Signed updates Bulletins, lore lines, and care/maintenance notices published by the season operator or place stewards.
  • Cooperation beats hoarding Some objects evolve only after the group shares clues outward — hiding information delays everyone’s next chapter.

What a scan does not prove

  • Who scanned No leaderboard, streak, XP, heatmap, or per-player scan history.
  • Legal identity or ownership Holding a sticker does not prove you are the card owner or a faction member.
  • Safety certification Game copy never overrides care streams. Players do not mark emergency equipment safe.

How to play (cooperation-first)

  • Public goods River Lantern–style nodes wake shared clues only after the city contributes together — first finder gets a seed, sharing completes the puzzle.
  • Sanctuaries Café windows and treaty benches are regroup zones — no capture, no faction grinding.
  • Trust paths Some doors need a witness seal or nearby vouch — legitimacy from place-linked objects, not phone numbers.
  • Finale lattice Fragments across Czech Village, Greene Square, and NewBo combine — solo optimization cannot flip the alley arch alone.
