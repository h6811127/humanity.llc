# Movement Narrative

**Status:** Strategic draft  
**Purpose:** Define the emotional and cultural layer of Humanity Commons without overloading the product UI or turning the card into "Linktree plus politics."

**Architecture (factual):** `docs/PROTOCOL_FEDERATION_AND_LAUNCH_STRATEGY.md` — open spec, federated networks, public card creation.

**Direction (factual):** `docs/DEMOCRATIC_INFRASTRUCTURE.md` — infrastructure for member-run communities, not a platform empire or blockchain identity play.

---

## Product And Movement Boundary

The product earns trust in five seconds. The movement gives people a reason to care for five years.

The public card and scan page should stay practical:

- Is this card active?
- Is this person vouched?
- Was live control proven?
- Is this QR active, revoked, suspended, expired, or unknown?
- What does this scan not prove?
- Is scan analytics disabled?

The movement belongs in:

- The homepage.
- The trust model page.
- Public launch and optional early tester pages.
- Artifact copy.
- Public launch posts.
- Events and ceremonies.
- The roadmap and governance pages.
- Supporter/membership materials.

The movement should make the product meaningful, not make the product harder to understand.

---

## Core Belief

Human trust should not require surveillance, platform lock-in, follower counts, data brokerage, legal identity exposure, or pay-to-be-real status.

Humanity Commons starts with a small object that must **grow into infrastructure**:

> A signed public card, vouched by real people, revocable by its owner, with scans that show **current** trust state—not a static profile—and readable without becoming another tracking surface.

The movement fails if the scan stays a vanity link. It wins if communities **check the same grammar** at the door (status, vouch, revoke, live control, then Commons Pass for orgs).

---

## What We Are Against

Humanity Commons is against:

- Platform-owned identity.
- Surveillance as the price of trust.
- Phone-number dependence.
- Government ID as the only path to legitimacy.
- Follower counts as social worth.
- Pay-to-verify status systems.
- Hidden trust scores.
- Silent scan analytics.
- Identity systems controlled by capital instead of users and workers.
- Treating people as growth inventory.

This should be communicated as a product stance, not as a purity test.

---

## What We Are For

Humanity Commons is for:

- Human trust without surveillance.
- Public claims that are inspectable and limited.
- Revocation as a user right.
- Export and exit.
- Vouching as accountable social trust.
- Live proof when static QR is not enough.
- Member-governed standards.
- Worker and user power over infrastructure.
- Beautiful physical-digital rituals that help people find each other.

---

## Why Now

The internet is entering a phase where accounts, bots, generated content, fake reputation, and platform-owned verification are becoming harder to distinguish.

Common responses push people toward:

- More surveillance.
- More legal identity checks.
- More centralized account providers.
- More platform dependence.
- More opaque reputation scoring.

Humanity Commons exists to test another path:

> Prove enough for the situation without surrendering more of yourself than the situation requires.

---

## Who This Is For First

Humanity Commons is not for everyone at launch.

It is first for communities that already feel the cost of extractive platforms:

- Founding events and meetups.
- Cooperatives.
- Mutual-aid groups.
- Unions and worker organizations.
- Privacy/open-source communities.
- Local clubs and community groups.
- Online communities facing AI/bot spam.
- Organizers who want human membership without phone or ID dependence.

The first audience should see Humanity Commons as useful, not merely agreeable.

## What Joining Means

Creating a Humanity Card means:

- You have a signed public card.
- You can share a QR that resolves to current status.
- You can be vouched for under published rules.
- You can prove live control when needed.
- You can revoke your card or item QR credentials.
- You can export your data.

Volunteering as an early tester (optional) may also mean:

- Testing vouching.
- Testing live proof.
- Reviewing copy comprehension.
- Reporting confusing trust claims.
- Helping ratify the early rules after using the product.
- Inviting trusted humans only when you understand the vouching responsibility.

Joining does not mean:

- You bought verification.
- You received legal identity status.
- You gained governance control before governance exists.
- You joined a social network.
- You agreed to surveillance.

---

## Movement Voice

The voice should be:

- Clear.
- Human.
- Calmly defiant.
- Technically honest.
- Anti-surveillance without being paranoid.
- Political in its institutional commitments, not in every UI sentence.
- Welcoming without pretending trust is easy.

Avoid:

- Grandiose claims.
- Revolutionary cosplay.
- Crypto-coded language.
- Government-ID aesthetics.
- Moral superiority.
- Dense ideological paragraphs in product flows.
- "We are saving humanity" rhetoric.

---

## Repeatable Lines

Primary:

> No phone. No ID. No ads. No tracking.

> A public card for real humans.

> Prove you're real without becoming a data product.

> A signed card. A real vouch. A right to revoke.

> Human trust without platform surveillance.

Longer:

> Humanity Commons lets you create a signed public card, get vouched for by real people, prove live control when needed, and carry a QR that always resolves to current status.

Movement:

> We are building trust infrastructure that communities can govern instead of another platform that owns people.

---

## Narrative Ladder

### Five Seconds

This is a public card for real humans.

### Thirty Seconds

Create a signed card, get vouched for, prove live control, and share a QR that resolves to current status without phone numbers, government ID, ads, or scan analytics.

### Three Minutes

Humanity Commons is a practical trust tool and a governance experiment. It starts founder-built, with public rules and **open card creation** when the network ships. If the trust loop works, it moves toward **federated operators** and member/worker governance so the infrastructure is accountable to the people who use and build it.

---

## Movement Principle

The movement should make people want to share the card. The product should make them glad they did.
