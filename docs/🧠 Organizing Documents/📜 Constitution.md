## Preamble

We the members of the Humanity Commons establish this system to enable direct, consensual connection between individuals through public profiles and QR codes.

We recognize that digital infrastructure shapes human relationships. We choose to build infrastructure that prioritizes:

- Individual agency over centralized control
    
- Transparency over hidden algorithms
    
- Portability over lock-in
    
- Consent over surveillance
    

This constitution sets the boundaries within which the technical system operates. It does not declare a political ideology. It declares engineering choices that protect human dignity.

---

## Article I – Sovereignty

**Section 1.1 – Ownership**  
A person's profile and QR mapping belong to that person. No platform, company, or collective may claim ownership of a user's identity representation.

**Section 1.2 – Portability**  
Any user may export their complete profile data and QR mapping in a standard, documented format. Export must be possible within 24 hours of request.

**Section 1.3 – Exit**  
Users may leave the system at any time. Leaving must not require permission, explanation, or payment.

---

## Article II – Consent

**Section 2.1 – Layered visibility**  
Profile information must be organized into distinct layers with different consent requirements:

- **Public layer:** Visible to anyone who scans the QR. Contains only what the user chooses to share with everyone.
    
- **Semi-public layer:** Visible only to viewers the user has explicitly approved.
    
- **Private layer:** Never visible via QR scan. Access requires mutual, explicit, revocable consent.
    

**Section 2.2 – No silent data collection**  
The system may not collect or transmit data about who scans which QR unless both the profile owner and the scanner have explicitly opted into logging.

**Section 2.3 – Right to revoke**  
Any user may revoke their QR profile instantly, without explanation. Revocation must take effect within one hour.

---

## Article III – Transparency

**Section 3.1 – Open standards**  
All technical standards governing QR format, resolution, and profile data must be publicly documented.

**Section 3.2 – Source availability**  
The reference implementation of the resolver and client software must be publicly available under an open source license.

**Section 3.3 – No hidden logic**  
No algorithm or automated decision system may determine who can see which profile content without documented, auditable rules.

---

## Article IV – Resilience

**Section 4.1 – No single point of control**  
The resolver network must consist of at least three independent operators in any region where the system operates. No single organization may control resolution.

**Section 4.2 – Offline operation**  
The system must function, in degraded mode, without continuous internet connectivity. Profile caches must enable offline viewing.

**Section 4.3 – Low-bandwidth design**  
The QR payload and profile data must be small enough to function on slow, unreliable, or expensive connections.

---

## Article V – Accountability

**Section 5.1 – User revocation**  
Users may revoke their own profile at any time. No further justification required.

**Section 5.2 – Community suspension**  
A profile may be suspended by collective action only under documented conditions:

- Verified impersonation
    
- Systematic harassment using the QR feature
    
- Distribution of illegal content as defined by the laws of the jurisdiction where the resolver operates (with appeal rights)
    

Suspension requires:

- Notice to the affected user
    
- An opportunity to appeal
    
- A documented decision by an elected accountability body
    

**Section 5.3 – No shadow actions**  
There is no "soft suspension," "shadow banning," or "limited visibility" state. A profile either resolves fully, shows a suspension notice, or returns no data.

---

## Article VI – Privacy

**Section 6.1 – No phone number requirement**  
Registration and profile creation must not require a phone number, email address, or government identifier.

**Section 6.2 – No tracking by default**  
Analytics, telemetry, and usage tracking must be disabled by default. If enabled by user choice, the scope and destination of data must be clearly disclosed.

**Section 6.3 – Encryption**  
Private keys must remain on user devices. The system may not store unencrypted private profile data on resolvers.

---

## Article VII – Governance

**Section 7.1 – Collective decision-making**  
Changes to this constitution or to the technical standards that affect user rights require collective ratification.

**Section 7.2 – Amendment process**  
Proposed amendments require:

- A 14-day public comment period
    
- A simple majority of participating members for most changes
    
- A 60% supermajority for changes to Articles I, II, or V (Sovereignty, Consent, Accountability)
    

**Section 7.3 – Emergency changes**  
Security vulnerabilities may be patched immediately. Emergency patches must be retroactively ratified within 30 days or they automatically expire.

---

## Article VIII – Limits

**Section 8.1 – This is not a legal entity**  
This constitution governs the technical and social rules of the Humanity Commons system. It is not a substitute for legal incorporation or formal dispute resolution.

**Section 8.2 – No binding authority over users**  
Users agree to these rules by participating. Enforcement is limited to suspension of profile resolution. The system has no authority over users' offline behavior.

**Section 8.3 – Jurisdiction**  
Resolvers operate within the laws of their physical locations. Users are responsible for their own legal compliance.

---

## Article IX – Amendment and Ratification

**Section 9.1 – Initial ratification**  
This constitution enters into effect upon adoption by the initial members of the Humanity Commons collective.

**Section 9.2 – Living document**  
This constitution may be amended as described in Article VII. Amendments are archived alongside the original text.

**Section 9.3 – Periodic review**  
Every two years, the collective must review this constitution for relevance and propose any necessary updates.
