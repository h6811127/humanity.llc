# Commons Pass Product Strategy

**Status:** Strategic draft  
**Purpose:** Reframe Humanity Commons from an individual QR identity card into community membership infrastructure for democratic groups, while preserving the Humanity Card as the personal credential primitive.

---

## Core Thesis

The empire is not a profile company.

The empire is:

> Membership infrastructure for the people’s internet.

Commons Pass is a mobile web membership pass for communities that want trust, belonging, events, credentials, and governance without surveillance platforms.

Humanity Card remains the personal identity and key-control primitive. Commons Pass is the product people adopt together.

## Why This Wedge Is Stronger

A standalone Humanity Card asks:

> Do you want a new identity object?

Commons Pass asks:

> Does your community need infrastructure?

That is a stronger wedge because communities already have painful workflows:

- Membership lists.
- Event check-in.
- Dues and donations.
- Volunteer coordination.
- Bot/fake account pressure.
- Trust and safety.
- Member legitimacy.
- Credential issuance.
- Contact continuity across platforms.
- Governance participation.
- Physical artifacts and fundraising.

The QR becomes memorable because it does something in the room:

- Scan to verify membership.
- Scan to check in.
- Scan to vouch after meeting.
- Scan to prove live control.
- Scan to receive a credential.
- Scan to join a campaign.
- Scan to access a member page.

## The Product Stack

### Humanity Card

Personal, signed, revocable card controlled by a human.

Answers:

- Who controls this card?
- Is it active?
- Is it vouched?
- Can the owner prove live control?

### Commons Pass

Community membership pass issued to a Humanity Card.

Answers:

- What community issued this pass?
- Is membership active?
- What roles, credentials, or stamps are public?
- Can the member prove live control?
- Can the member check in or receive new stamps?

### Community Console

Operator surface for community admins/stewards.

Answers:

- Who are members?
- Who has been invited?
- Who checked in?
- Which credentials were issued?
- Which passes are suspended/revoked?
- What exports and audit logs exist?

## Target Customers And Users

### Primary Early Customer

Community organizers who run groups with real membership or recurring gatherings.

Examples:

- Cooperatives.
- Unions and worker groups.
- Mutual-aid networks.
- Clubs.
- Artist collectives.
- Open-source communities.
- Privacy communities.
- Local political or civic groups.
- Conferences and recurring meetups.
- Community spaces.

### Primary End User

A person who belongs to one or more communities and wants portable, revocable proof of that belonging without platform dependence.

### Buyer/Funder

Likely early payers:

- Community operators.
- Event organizers.
- Cooperatives.
- Grant funders.
- Supporters.
- Member-funded organizations.

## First Beachhead

Recommended first beachhead:

> Events and founding communities that need membership, check-in, and trust rituals.

Why:

- QR scanning is natural.
- Live control proof is understandable.
- Vouching can happen after real contact.
- Physical cards/stickers have a purpose.
- The demo is memorable.

First use case:

> A community creates a Commons Pass for a founding event. Members receive mobile web passes, scan each other, prove live control, check in, and receive a signed attendance or founding-member stamp.

---

## Viral Loops

### Community Invite Loop

Organizer creates community -> invites members -> members receive passes -> members invite other trusted members.

### Event Check-In Loop

People scan passes at events -> attendees see the product in use -> new communities ask for their own pass.

### Stamp Loop

Communities issue memorable stamps -> members want to collect and display them -> other communities want to issue their own.

### Vouch Loop

Members vouch for known humans -> vouched humans invite others -> trust graph grows carefully.

### Artifact Loop

Physical cards/stickers make the pass visible -> scans lead to community pages -> people join or ask how to create their own community.

### Movement Loop

The line spreads:

> No phone. No ID. No ads. No tracking.

## The Magical Moment

The strongest demo:

1. A person opens a Commons Pass in Safari.
2. Scanner sees community membership and current status.
3. Scanner taps `Ask for live proof`.
4. Pass holder signs a challenge.
5. Scanner sees `Control proven moments ago`.
6. Organizer scans the same pass to check the person into an event.
7. The pass receives an event stamp.

This is creative, memorable, and operationally useful.

---

## V1 Product Promise

> Create a community, invite members, issue mobile web passes, scan QR codes for current membership status, check people into events, and issue signed community stamps without phone numbers, ads, or scan analytics.

---

## V1 Non-Goals

Do not build in V1:

- Full social feed.
- Public global directory.
- Marketplace.
- Complex voting system.
- Native mobile app.
- Legal identity verification.
- KYC/age verification.
- Token/NFT ownership.
- Arbitrary third-party plugin platform.
- Complex multi-chapter federation.

---

## Success Criteria

V1 is working if:

- One real community creates a Commons Pass.
- 25 members receive passes.
- Members can scan each other on mobile Safari.
- Organizer can check members into one event.
- Community can issue one signed stamp.
- At least 10 members understand what the pass proves and does not prove.
- At least 5 members share or invite others.
- The community wants to use it again.

---

## Strategic Verdict

Commons Pass is the more empire-worthy wedge.

Humanity Card is still important, but it should become the personal credential inside a broader system for collective life.

The product should begin with a community pass, not a generic identity card.
